# Conversion Benchmarks for DM Automations

Performance benchmarks for DM automation flows. Use these to set targets, diagnose underperformance, and prove ROI.

---

## The Core Comparison: DMs vs Email

| Metric | DM Automation | Email Marketing | Difference |
|--------|--------------|-----------------|------------|
| Open rate | 80-90% | 18-22% | 4x higher |
| Click-through rate | 20-40% | 2-5% | 8-10x higher |
| Response rate | 30-50% | 1-3% | 15-20x higher |
| Time to open | Under 3 minutes | 4-6 hours | 80-100x faster |
| Unsubscribe friction | Low (just stop replying) | Requires link click | DMs feel less committal |

DMs win because they live in the same app people check 50+ times a day. Email sits in a box people dread opening.

## Button Click Rates by Position

| Button Position | Click Rate | Notes |
|-----------------|-----------|-------|
| First button (top) | 40-60% | This is your primary CTA. Make it the action you want most. |
| Second button | 20-30% | Good for a secondary option or "Tell me more" |
| Third button | 8-15% | Diminishing returns. Only include if truly needed. |
| Fourth+ button | Under 5% | Almost never clicked. Remove or restructure. |

### Thresholds

| Metric | Good | Average | Needs Work |
|--------|------|---------|------------|
| First button CTR | 50%+ | 35-50% | Below 35% |
| Second button CTR | 25%+ | 15-25% | Below 15% |
| Overall button engagement | 60%+ | 40-60% | Below 40% |

## Quick Reply Response Rates

Quick replies (pre-filled response bubbles) outperform buttons in conversational flows because they feel like the user is "talking."

| Quick Reply Count | Response Rate | Notes |
|-------------------|--------------|-------|
| 2 options | 55-70% | Best for yes/no or binary choices |
| 3 options | 45-60% | Sweet spot for most qualification questions |
| 4 options | 30-45% | Acceptable for quiz-style flows |
| 5+ options | Under 25% | Too many choices. Split into two messages. |

## User Input Completion Rates

When the flow asks users to type something (free text, email, phone).

| Input Type | Completion Rate | Notes |
|-----------|----------------|-------|
| First name | 60-75% | Low friction. People are used to this. |
| Email address | 30-50% | Major drop-off point. Frame as "Where should I send it?" |
| Phone number | 15-25% | Highest friction. Only ask if essential. Never ask first. |
| Open-ended question | 40-55% | Works well mid-flow when user is invested |
| Multi-step form (3+ fields) | 15-30% | Each additional field drops completion 10-15% |

### Thresholds

| Metric | Good | Average | Needs Work |
|--------|------|---------|------------|
| Email collection | 45%+ | 30-45% | Below 30% |
| Phone collection | 22%+ | 15-22% | Below 15% |
| Open-ended response | 50%+ | 35-50% | Below 35% |

## Flow Completion Rates by Type

Percentage of users who enter the flow and reach the final step.

| Flow Type | Completion Rate | Typical Steps | Notes |
|-----------|----------------|---------------|-------|
| Lead magnet delivery | 40-60% | 3-5 | Highest completion because value is immediate |
| Quiz / assessment | 30-50% | 5-8 | Engagement stays high if questions feel personalized |
| Sales qualifier | 25-40% | 4-7 | Drop-off increases with each qualifying question |
| Booking flow | 20-35% | 5-8 | Calendar link is the biggest friction point |
| Full application | 15-25% | 8-12 | Only use for high-ticket offers where friction is intentional |
| Re-engagement | 10-20% | 2-3 | Cold audiences have low intent |

### Thresholds

| Flow Type | Good | Average | Needs Work |
|-----------|------|---------|------------|
| Lead magnet | 55%+ | 40-55% | Below 40% |
| Quiz | 45%+ | 30-45% | Below 30% |
| Sales qualifier | 35%+ | 25-35% | Below 25% |
| Booking | 30%+ | 20-30% | Below 20% |

## Show Rates for Booking Flows

| Scenario | Show Rate | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| DM booking + reminder sequence | 60-80% | Best case. DM reminders feel personal. |
| DM booking, no reminders | 40-55% | Significant drop without follow-up |
| Email booking + email reminders | 30-50% | Standard but lower engagement |
| Cold traffic booking | 25-40% | Lower intent from the start |

### Reminder Sequence That Works

| Timing | Message | Show Rate Impact |
|--------|---------|-----------------|
| Immediately after booking | Confirmation + what to expect | Baseline |
| 24 hours before | Reminder + prep instructions | +10-15% |
| 1 hour before | "See you in 60 minutes" + meeting link | +5-10% |
| 5 minutes before | "Starting now" + link | +3-5% |

## Re-engagement Response Rates

| Timing After Last Interaction | Response Rate | Notes |
|-------------------------------|--------------|-------|
| 1-3 hours | 25-40% | Still warm. Best window for abandoned flow recovery. |
| 24 hours | 15-25% | Good for "Did you still want this?" |
| 48-72 hours | 10-18% | Needs a new hook or offer |
| 7+ days | 5-12% | Treat as a cold re-activation |
| 30+ days | 2-5% | Very low. Only worth it for large lists. |

### Thresholds

| Timing | Good | Average | Needs Work |
|--------|------|---------|------------|
| Under 24hr re-engage | 30%+ | 20-30% | Below 20% |
| 24-72hr re-engage | 18%+ | 10-18% | Below 10% |
| 7+ day re-engage | 10%+ | 5-10% | Below 5% |

## Speed to Respond

| Response Time | Conversion Impact |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Under 1 minute | Highest conversion. User is still in-app, still engaged. |
| 1-5 minutes | 8x more likely to convert than 30+ minute response |
| 5-30 minutes | Moderate. User may have moved on but can be pulled back. |
| 30-60 minutes | Significant drop. Feels like talking to a wall. |
| 1+ hours | Most of the urgency is gone. Lead is cooling fast. |
| 24+ hours | Effectively a cold lead again. |

Automation wins here by default. A well-built flow responds in under 1 second, every time, 24/7.

## Platform Comparison: Instagram vs Messenger vs WhatsApp

| Metric | Instagram DMs | Messenger | WhatsApp |
|--------|--------------|-----------|----------|
| Open rate | 80-88% | 82-90% | 90-95% |
| Response rate | 35-50% | 30-45% | 50-65% |
| Button CTR | 40-55% | 35-50% | 45-60% |
| Email collection | 30-45% | 35-50% | 40-55% |
| 24hr window | Strict | Strict | Strict (template messages outside) |
| Media support | Images, video, links | Images, video, links, webviews | Images, video, documents, location |
| Best for | Organic content funnels | Ad-to-DM funnels | High-intent leads, international |

### Key Differences

- **Instagram** has the best organic reach. Comment automations drive the most volume.
- **Messenger** pairs naturally with Facebook ads. Ads JSON trigger works seamlessly.
- **WhatsApp** has the highest open and response rates globally but requires template message approval for messages outside the 24-hour window.
- **WhatsApp Business API** costs per conversation. Factor this into unit economics.

## Mobile vs Desktop Behavior

| Behavior | Mobile (85-90% of DM traffic) | Desktop (10-15%) |
|----------|-------------------------------|-------------------|
| Response speed | Under 2 minutes average | 5-15 minutes average |
| Session length | 1-3 minutes | 3-8 minutes |
| Typing tolerance | Low. Keep inputs short. | Moderate. Can handle longer forms. |
| Button preference | Strong. Thumbs tap easily. | Moderate. Also comfortable typing. |
| Link click-through | Lower (leaving the app feels risky) | Higher (new tab is painless) |
| Video viewing | High (autoplay in-app) | Moderate |

Design for mobile first. Always. If it works on a phone screen, it works everywhere.

## How to Use These Benchmarks

1. **Set targets before building.** Pick the flow type, find the "Good" threshold, design to hit it.
2. **Measure after 100+ users.** Anything under 100 is noise. Wait for statistical significance.
3. **Diagnose with the funnel.** If completion is low, find the exact step where users drop off. Fix that step.
4. **Compare to YOUR baseline.** Industry benchmarks are starting points. Your audience may behave differently.
5. **Re-benchmark quarterly.** Platform algorithms change. User behavior shifts. Keep your numbers current.
